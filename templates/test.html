About Us


For a Better Future


The goal of this project is to create an autonomous shuttle for the urban Indian last mile. The shuttle will operate on predefined routes and provide hassle free anytime connectivity to modes of public transport like the Metro. To keep the costs per shuttle low, we plan to leverage state-of-the-art Artificial Intelligence and other computational techniques that enable reducing the costs of sensing hardware needed per shuttle. In its first avatar the shuttle will provide connectivity between points in a university campus.

With this in mind, over the next year, we plan to achieve autonomous static obstacle avoidance, lane following, reading of basic traffic signals, and following speed limits. For doing this, during the learning phase, the shuttle builds a 3-D map of the route it will take, and then uses it for navigation.

Our current choice of vehicle platform is an electric rickshaw, and currently we are working on providing shuttle service within the IIIT-Delhi campus. We also hope to replicate the above on Mahindraâ€™s latest electric car, the e2o.

Implemented Software


These Are Some Of The Softwares And Technology Used In Our Car.

Lane Detection

Detecting roads from their lane markings (white lines) on the roads.

Curb Detection

Is used to detect the curbs on the side of the roads

Traffic Light Detection

For better real-world usage and better autonomy

Obstacle Detection

Used to detect obstacles on the road and avoid them by getting out of their way

Scan Matching

Uses the scans and the maps to find the rigid-body transformation (translation+rotation) that aligns them best

Implemented Hardware


These Are Some Of The Harware Used In Our Car.

Velodyne LiDAR

Used to create a full 360 degree environmental view for 3D-Mapping and better navigation

Pointgrey Camera

For capturing roads and curbs for further detection and processing

Can Interface

Controlled Area Network which allows the ROS Codes and the hardware of the automobile to interact with each other. It is a message-based protocol

SLAM

The method of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location within it
